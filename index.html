About Me

Scholar,
Educator & Researcher

I am an Associate Professor in the Department of Computer Science, where I have been a faculty member since 2014. My work sits at the intersection of computational linguistics, cognitive science, and machine learning.

Before joining here, I was a postdoctoral researcher at MIT CSAIL and earned my PhD from IIT Delhi under Prof. Rajiv Kumar. I believe in research that is rigorous, reproducible, and relevant to real human needs.

Research Areas

What I Work On

🧠

Cognitive NLP

Understanding language comprehension through computational models that mirror human reading and parsing behaviour.

🌐

Low-Resource Languages

Building robust NLP tools for under-resourced Indian languages using transfer learning and cross-lingual models.

🤖

Human-AI Dialogue

Designing conversational agents that maintain coherent, contextually appropriate, and ethically grounded interactions.

📖

Discourse & Pragmatics

Computational approaches to understanding how meaning arises from context, inference, and conversational implicature.

🔬

Language Acquisition

Using corpora and neural models to study how children acquire grammatical structure from noisy input.

📊

Evaluation Methods

Developing better benchmarks and metrics for assessing the linguistic quality and fairness of language models.
